PROGRAM: Toshiba America Foundation – Grades 6-8 Science & STEM Grants

SUMMARY:
Toshiba America Foundation (TAF) provides grants to help middle school teachers bring innovative hands-on science and STEM learning to their classrooms. Funding supports projects that make science engaging, inquiry-based, and aligned with real-world problem solving.

FUNDING PRIORITIES:
• Hands-on, inquiry-based science and engineering projects
• Classroom activities that deepen scientific understanding
• Use of technology or lab equipment to support STEM learning
• Projects that integrate engineering design challenges
• Initiatives that improve student engagement and curiosity in science

AWARD AMOUNT:
Up to $5,000 per project for Grades 6–8

ELIGIBILITY:
• Public or private schools in the United States
• Teachers of Grades 6–8 science, technology, engineering, or math
• Must submit a project plan that directly benefits students
• Nonprofit organizations working with middle school STEM programs may also be eligible

POPULATIONS SERVED:
• Middle school students (Grades 6–8)
• Underserved or under-resourced classrooms are encouraged to apply

GEOGRAPHIC FOCUS:
United States

EXCLUSIONS:
• After-school programs not led by certified teachers
• Professional development for teachers
• General operating expenses
• Projects not focused specifically on science or STEM education

APPLICATION PROCESS:
Applications accepted twice per year. Educators must submit a project description, list of materials, expected learning outcomes, and project timeline. No administrator approval required at initial submission.

NOTES:
Ideal for schools introducing robotics, engineering design, lab equipment, environmental science investigations, or physics/chemistry demonstrations.
