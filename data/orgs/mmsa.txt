MMSA — Organizational Profile (For Grant-Matching Test)

Organization Overview

The Maine Math & Science Alliance (MMSA) is a statewide STEM education nonprofit dedicated to improving how science, technology, engineering, and mathematics are taught and experienced across Maine and beyond. Founded in 1992 with support from the National Science Foundation, MMSA has become a leader in designing innovative STEM learning models, strengthening educator capacity, and generating research-backed improvements in STEM teaching.

MMSA has received more than $34 million in grants and philanthropic funding to support its work, and collaborates with schools, districts, state agencies, higher education institutions, and national partners to expand STEM opportunities.

⸻

Mission Statement

MMSA’s mission is to inspire meaningful and engaging STEM learning experiences so that all youth—regardless of background or geography—can become the innovators, problem-solvers, and workforce of tomorrow.

⸻

Program Areas / Core Activities

MMSA’s work spans three major strategic functions:

1. Professional Development
	•	Designs and delivers high-quality STEM professional learning experiences
	•	Supports teachers in implementing engaging, inquiry-based STEM instruction
	•	Builds educator confidence and pedagogical skill across K–12 classrooms

2. Research & Evaluation
	•	Conducts research on STEM learning experiences
	•	Evaluates STEM education initiatives to identify effective models
	•	Translates findings into actionable insights for schools and systems

3. Relationship & Network Building
	•	Fosters partnerships across Maine and nationally
	•	Develops networks that sustain statewide STEM improvement
	•	Supports community-based and rural STEM engagement efforts

⸻

History & Organizational Background

MMSA was established in 1992 as a 501(c)(3) nonprofit with initial support from the National Science Foundation. Over three decades, MMSA has expanded its work statewide and nationally through major grants, multi-year research collaborations, and STEM innovation initiatives. The organization celebrated its 30th anniversary with a retrospective on its impact and long-term STEM education contributions.

⸻

Commitment to Accessibility & Equity

MMSA is committed to equitable access to STEM learning, working to ensure that all students—especially those in rural or underserved communities—have meaningful opportunities to explore STEM.
The organization centers diversity, equity, inclusion, and accessibility (DEIA) in its programs, decision-making, and partnerships.

Grant Purpose Overview (Generic Template for Testing)

MMSA is seeking grant support to scale educator training, expand rural and underserved STEM access, and strengthen research that drives systemic statewide improvement in STEM education.

Funding will support:
	1.	New, research-backed STEM professional development programs
	2.	Evaluation and refinement of STEM teaching models
	3.	Expansion of regional and statewide STEM educator networks
	4.	Equity-driven STEM initiatives for rural and underrepresented students
	5.	Dissemination of findings, toolkits, and best practices

Grant Summary Statement (Final Paragraph)

MMSA requests funding to deepen its impact in rural and underserved communities, strengthen educator capacity through high-quality STEM professional development, and accelerate research-driven improvements in STEM education across Maine and the nation. This support will empower educators and expand access to engaging, equitable STEM opportunities for all students.
