Khan Academy — Organizational Profile (For Grant-Matching Test)

Organization Overview

Khan Academy is a global education nonprofit dedicated to providing a free, world-class education for anyone, anywhere. Founded in 2008, Khan Academy offers an extensive library of mastery-based learning materials, including instructional videos, practice exercises, assessments, and personalized learning pathways across math, science, humanities, economics, computing, and test preparation.

Khan Academy serves over 150 million learners in 190+ countries and partners with school districts, states, and ministries of education to support high-impact, data-driven classroom instruction.

⸻

Mission Statement

Khan Academy’s mission is to democratize access to high-quality education by offering free, personalized learning tools that support learners of all ages—regardless of background, income, or geography.

⸻

Program Areas / Core Activities

Khan Academy’s work centers on four main activity areas:

1. Mastery-Based Learning Platform
	•	Free digital learning platform covering K–12 math, science, humanities, and computing
	•	Personalized learning pathways driven by analytics and progress data
	•	Adaptive practice and AI-supported tutoring features

2. Teacher & Classroom Support
	•	Dashboard tools for teachers to track mastery, assign content, and differentiate instruction
	•	Curricular alignment to state and national standards
	•	Professional learning and implementation support for school and district partners

3. Large-Scale Education System Partnerships
	•	Partnerships with states, districts, and countries to deploy Khan Academy at scale
	•	Customized content alignment and deployment programs
	•	Research collaborations to measure student learning outcomes

4. Research & Learning Innovation
	•	Learning science research on mastery learning, feedback loops, and instructional design
	•	Development of AI-enhanced learning tools such as Khanmigo, the AI tutoring assistant
	•	Continuous experimentation to explore the future of personalized learning

⸻

History & Impact

Founded by Sal Khan, Khan Academy began as a series of online tutoring videos and rapidly expanded into one of the most trusted digital learning platforms in the world.
The organization works across formal and informal learning environments and has been adopted widely during COVID-19, remote learning transitions, and pandemic recovery initiatives.

Khan Academy also operates specialized programs, including:
	•	Khan Academy Kids (early childhood learning)
	•	Khan Lab School (experimental mastery-based school)
	•	District partnerships supporting high-poverty, multilingual, and underserved communities

⸻

Commitment to Equity & Accessibility

Khan Academy is deeply committed to:
	•	Bridging achievement gaps
	•	Serving low-income and historically underserved students
	•	Supporting multilingual learners
	•	Ensuring accessibility across devices, including mobile and low-bandwidth environments
	•	Providing all content free of charge, forever

Accessibility and universal design principles are embedded across product development.

Grant Purpose Overview (Generic Testing Template)

Khan Academy is seeking funding to expand equitable access to free, personalized, mastery-based learning tools, accelerate integration of AI-supported tutoring, and deepen partnerships with schools and districts serving historically marginalized learners.

Funding will support:
	1.	Scaling Khanmigo and AI tutoring tools to underserved schools
	2.	Developing new mastery-based content in core STEM subjects
	3.	Expanding multilingual and accessibility features
	4.	Supporting district-level implementation, teacher training, and community engagement
	5.	Research on learning science, AI-enabled instruction, and student mastery outcomes

Grant Summary Statement (Final Paragraph)

Khan Academy requests support to accelerate the next generation of free, personalized learning technology and ensure that every learner—especially those in historically underserved communities—can access high-quality education. Funding will advance mastery-based instruction, AI-powered tutoring, district partnerships, and equity-focused learning innovations.
